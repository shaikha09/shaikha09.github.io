Students Guide for a Healthy Lifestyle

A healthy lifestyle is essential for students to perform well academically, stay energized, and maintain overall well-being.



1) Maintaing a balanced diet: Prioritize a well-balanced diet with a variety of fruits, vegetables, whole grains, lean proteins, and healthy fats. While being on campus, processed foods may seem easier and quicker for students to grab on the go. However, consuming processed foods, sugary, snacks, and fast foods can cause fatigue, low-levels energy, and weigth gain. Therefore meal prepping,incorporating all food groups, and staying hydrated is important in keeping students healthy and focused in their academics.






2) Exercising: Exercising daily or 3-5 times a week is super important for students, not only because they remain seated for a long period of time but it also releases stress. Include activities you enjoy, such as walking, jogging, dancing, or team sports can be helpful way for students to stay active. NYUAD offers a gym and equipments for students and dedicates time for womans only.




3) Sleep: Prioritizing a sleeping schedule is needed so that students remain energetic and focused in their academics. By establishing a sleeping schedule and getting atleast 7-9 hours of sleep helps the physical and mental health of students.





4)Setting Goals: By setting goals for your academic and personal life can motivate you to accomplish things. Breaking big tasks into smaller ones van help with procastionation and laziness especially during stressful times and deadlines.






5) Reaching out: Asking for help can be intimidating but it is very helpful. NYUAD values Mental and physical health and has plenty of resources from counselors, academic advisors, or healthcare professionals if you face challenges.




Remember that a healthy lifestyle is a journey, and it's okay to make gradual changes. Prioritize self-care, and don't be too hard on yourself if you slip up occasionally. Small, sustainable changes can lead to significant improvements in your overall health and well-being!
